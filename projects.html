2022 Piedmont Maker's Faire

For this project, I combined my interests in computer science, physics, and golf.

The purpose of this project was to create a device that could record the first meter of the golf ball's flight and from that data determine the trajectory of the ball's flight. There are several components to the project. First there is a laser, which when blocked (in the correct sequence) triggers camera to turn on. The LEDs that flash the ball to create more higher resolution images. All components are connected to a raspberry pi. When the program finishes, a plot of the calculated trajectory of the ball is displayed.

The trajectory of the ball is calculated using a numerical solution involving fluid dynamics and projectile motion.The forces acting on the ball are lift, drag, and gravity. However, lift and drag are dependent on the velocity of the ball at any point of time. To solve for the trajectory, we must know the innitial launch angle, velocity, and spin-rate of the ball (wich are determined using image recognition using data detected by the camera). After knowing the initial launch angle, velocity, and spin-rate, we must sequentialy itterate through each value of time, suming forces and using constants to find the acceleration of the ball.
